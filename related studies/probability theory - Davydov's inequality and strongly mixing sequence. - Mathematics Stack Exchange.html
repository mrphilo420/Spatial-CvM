Davydov's inequality and strongly mixing sequence.

Ask Question

Let {𝑋𝑖}{Xi} be an 𝛼α-mixing random process with coefficients 𝛼(𝑘)α(k) satisfying 𝛼(𝑘)≤𝐶𝑎𝑘α(k)≤Cak for some positive constants 𝑎<1a<1 and 𝐶C. Given that for any 𝑖∈ℕi∈N, 𝐸|𝑋𝑖|𝑞≤𝐶E|Xi|q≤C and 𝐸|𝑋𝑖|𝑟≤𝐶E|Xi|r≤C by some constant 𝐶>0C>0, Davydov's inequality states that

|Cov(𝑋𝑗,𝑋𝑖)|≤𝐶𝛼(|𝑖−𝑗|)1−1/𝑞−1/𝑟.|Cov⁡(Xj,Xi)|≤Cα(|i−j|)1−1/q−1/r.

Now, let the index 𝑖i be such that ∃𝑀>0:|𝑖−𝑙|>𝑀,∀𝑙∈{𝑖′,𝑗,𝑗′}∃M>0:|i−l|>M,∀l∈{i′,j,j′}. Why the following inequality is valid, using Davydov's result:

|Cov(𝑋𝑖,𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′)|≤𝐶𝛼(𝑀)1−1/𝑞−1/𝑟?|Cov⁡(Xi,Xi′XjXj′)|≤Cα(M)1−1/q−1/r?
I do not know how to deal with the random variable 𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′Xi′XjXj′ and its sigma-algebra generated. If anyone knows about mixing properties, then please, give me advices!

*obs. If 𝜎(𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′)=𝜎(𝑋𝑙:𝑙∈{𝑖′,𝑗,𝑗′})σ(Xi′XjXj′)=σ(Xl:l∈{i′,j,j′}), then I can obtain the result. However, I don't think that a sequence of random variables generates the same sigma-algebra as its product.

Thanks in advance.

Indeed, in general, the equality 𝜎(𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′)=𝜎(𝑋𝑙:𝑙∈{𝑖′,𝑗,𝑗′})σ(Xi′XjXj′)=σ(Xl:l∈{i′,j,j′}) may not hold. However, the inclusion

𝜎(𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′)⊂𝜎(𝑋𝑙:𝑙∈{𝑖′,𝑗,𝑗′})σ(Xi′XjXj′)⊂σ(Xl:l∈{i′,j,j′})
holds, because letting 𝑓(𝑥,𝑦,𝑧)=𝑥𝑦𝑧f(x,y,z)=xyz, the equality hold {𝑋𝑖′𝑋𝑗𝑋𝑗′∈𝐵}={(𝑋𝑖′,𝑋𝑗,𝑋𝑗′)∈𝑓−1(𝐵)}{Xi′XjXj′∈B}={(Xi′,Xj,Xj′)∈f−1(B)} and the latter is 𝜎(𝑋𝑙:𝑙∈{𝑖′,𝑗,𝑗′})σ(Xl:l∈{i′,j,j′})-measurable.
