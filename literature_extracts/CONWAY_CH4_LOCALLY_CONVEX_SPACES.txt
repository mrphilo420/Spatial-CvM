## Critical Theorems for SpatialCvM De-axiomatization

### **Theorem 1.1 (Definition of TVS and LCS)** — Pages 100-101
Topological vector space framework with seminorm topology.

**Key Definitions**:
- **Definition 1.2**: A locally convex space (LCS) is a TVS whose topology is defined by a family of seminorms $\mathscr{P}$ such that:
  $$\bigcap_{p \in \mathscr{P}} \{x : p(x) = 0\} = (0)$$

### **Theorem 1.3 — Page 101**
**Continuity of Seminorms**

For a TVS $\mathscr{X}$ and seminorm $p$, the following are equivalent:
1. (a) $p$ is continuous
2. (b) $\{x : p(x) < 1\}$ is open
3. (c) $0 \in \text{int}\{x : p(x) < 1\}$
4. (d) $0 \in \text{int}\{x : p(x) \leq 1\}$
5. (e) $p$ is continuous at 0
6. (f) There exists continuous seminorm $q$ with $p \leq q$

**Application**: Proving continuity of integral operators with Mercer kernels

---

### **Theorem 1.14 — Page 103**
**Minkowski Function (Gauge)**

If $\mathscr{X}$ is a vector space and $V$ is a nonempty convex, balanced set absorbing at each point, then there is a unique seminorm $p$ on $\mathscr{X}$ such that:
$$V = \{x \in \mathscr{X} : p(x) < 1\}$$

**Application**: Constructing seminorms from neighborhoods (needed for topology on function spaces)

---

### **Theorem 2.1 — Page 105**
**Metrization of LCS**

Let $\{p_1, p_2, \ldots\}$ be seminorms with $\bigcap_n \{x : p_n(x) = 0\} = (0)$. Define:
$$d(x, y) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty 2^{-n} \frac{p_n(x-y)}{1 + p_n(x-y)}$$

Then $d$ is a metric inducing the topology defined by $\{p_n\}$.

**Key Insight**: LCS is metrizable iff topology is determined by **countable** family of seminorms.

**Application**: Metric on $\ell^\infty[0,1]$ (though sup norm already does this)

---

### **Definition 2.4 — Page 106**
**Fréchet Space**

A Fréchet space is a TVS $\mathscr{X}$ whose topology is defined by a translation-invariant metric $d$ and $(\mathscr{X}, d)$ is complete.

**Application**: The space $C[0,1]$ with sup norm is a Fréchet space (actually a Banach space, hence complete)

---

### **Theorem 2.6 — Page 107**
**Normability Criterion**

**Proposition**: A LCS $\mathscr{X}$ is normable iff $\mathscr{X}$ has a nonempty **bounded** open set.

**Application**: Shows why weak topologies on $C[0,1]$ are not normable (no bounded open sets)

---

### **Theorem 3.3 — Page 109**
**Geometric Hahn-Banach (First Form)**

If $\mathscr{X}$ is a TVS and $G$ is an open convex nonempty set not containing 0, then there exists a closed hyperplane $\mathscr{M}$ such that $\mathscr{M} \cap G = \emptyset$.

**Application**: Separating convex sets (needed for dual spaces)

---

### **Theorem 3.9 — Page 111**
**Strict Separation Theorem** (THIS IS THE CRITICAL ONE)

**Theorem**: Let $\mathscr{X}$ be a real LCS and let $A$ and $B$ be two disjoint closed convex subsets. If $B$ is **compact**, then $A$ and $B$ are **strictly separated**.

That is, there exists continuous linear $f : \mathscr{X} \to \mathbb{R}$ and $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$, $\epsilon > 0$ such that:
$$\sup_{a \in A} f(a) \leq \alpha < \alpha + \epsilon \leq \inf_{b \in B} f(b)$$

**Proof Sketch**: Uses Lemma 3.8 (compact + open covering).

**Application**: 
- **Prokhorov's Theorem**: Relating compactness in probability measures
- **Tightness**: Shows when sets of probability measures can be separated

---

### **Corollary 3.10 — Page 111**
**Separation of Point from Convex Set**

If $\mathscr{X}$ is a real LCS, $A$ is a closed convex subset, and $x \notin A$, then $x$ is strictly separated from $A$.

---

### **Theorem 3.13 — Page 112**
**Complex Separation**

For complex LCS $\mathscr{X}$ with disjoint closed convex $A, B$ and $B$ compact, there exists continuous linear $f : \mathscr{X} \to \mathbb{C}$, $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$, $\epsilon > 0$ such that:
$$\text{Re } f(a) \leq \alpha < \alpha + \epsilon \leq \text{Re } f(b)$$

---

### **Theorem 4.1 — Page 114**
**Riesz Representation for C(X)**

If $X$ is completely regular and $L : C(X) \to \mathbb{F}$ is a continuous linear functional, then there exists:
- Compact set $K \subset X$
- Regular Borel measure $\mu$ on $K$

Such that:
$$L(f) = \int_K f \, d\mu$$

**Application**: Representing probability measures on $C[0,1]$ (Prokhorov's Theorem)

---

### **Theorem 5.7 — Page 119**
**Continuity of Linear Maps on Inductive Limits**

If $(\mathscr{X}, \mathscr{T})$ is the inductive limit of $\{(\mathscr{X}_i, \mathscr{T}_i)\}$ and $\mathscr{Y}$ is a LCS with $T : \mathscr{X} \to \mathscr{Y}$ linear, then $T$ is continuous iff $T|_{\mathscr{X}_i}$ is $\mathscr{T}_i$-continuous for each $i$.

**Application**: Showing continuity of functionals on strict inductive limits (distributions)

---

### **Proposition 5.15 — Page 120**
**Hausdorff Strict Inductive Limit**

If $\mathscr{X}$ is the strict inductive limit of $\{\mathscr{X}_n\}$, then:
1. The inductive limit topology is **Hausdorff**
2. The topology on $\mathscr{X}$ when restricted to $\mathscr{X}_k$ equals the original topology of $\mathscr{X}_k$

**Application**: Test function spaces $\mathscr{D}(\Omega)$ (distributions)
