Summary. It is shown that for p-mixing arrays of Banach space valued 
random vectors the central limit theorem implies the invariance principle. 
Applying this result to lattices of random variables a higher dimensional 
invariance principle under dependence assumptions is obtained. 
1. Introduction 
Under the classical assumptions that the random variables are independent and 
identically distributed the central limit theorem implies the invariance prin- 
ciple. This fact has been shown by Kuelbs [10] for triangular arrays 
{Xn,j}l<j<n.~__> 1 of random vectors taking values in a separable Banach space. 
Garling [67 pushed the result further showing that the entries in each row of the 
array need not be identically distributed provided second moments are finite 
and a Lindeberg condition holds. It is the purpose of this paper to drop the 
other assumption, namely independence and to apply the result obtained to 
lattices of dependent real-valued random variables. 
As we shall see in Sect. 3 basicly the same statement remains true for ~o- 
mixing arrays. But instead of assuming only convergence of the row sums Xn, 
+...+Xn. ~ we shall need in general convergence of all subsums Xn, l+... 
+X,.Ln,j where ue]0, 1]. Only if in addition a weak moment condition holds 
then convergence of the row sums itself will be enough. As a side result coming 
out of these considerations we shall state slight extensions of two results of 
Parthasarathy [12] concerning conditional compactness of sequences of proba- 
bility measures. 
In Sect. 4 we consider the stochastic process defined by linear interpolation 
of partial sums of a lattice of real-valued random variables, We restrict our 
attention to two-dimensional lattices in order to simplify the notation. Follow- 
ing an idea of Kuelbs [10J we transform the two-dimensional process in an one-
